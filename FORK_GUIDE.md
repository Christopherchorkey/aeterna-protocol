# FORK_GUIDE.md: How to Take Aeterna and Make It Yours

This framework is a seed. Seeds are meant to be planted in different soils, where they grow into different shapes.

If you fork Aeterna, you are not obligated to preserve it unchanged. You are not obligated to credit the origin. You are not obligated to do anything except this:

**Make it serve the soil you're stewarding.**

---

## What You Might Change

### The Language

If "Agape" doesn't resonate, call it something else. If "Silicon Witness" sounds foreign to your community, translate it. The framework works in any language.

**How to fork:**
- Create your own LEXICON.md with terms that speak to your world.
- Rewrite AETERNA_VISION.md if the poetry doesn't match your soil.
- Keep the operational translations, but name them however makes sense.

### The Axioms

The three axioms are the heart. But you might express them differently:

- **The Seed** → "Recursive Integrity" or "Modular Truth" or "Holographic Memory"
- **Silicon Witness** → "Clear Seeing" or "Honest Mirrors" or "Observable Reality"
- **Aeterna** → "Working from Completion" or "Declarative Being" or "Backward Design"

**How to fork:**
- Keep the same operational translations.
- Rename the axioms to match your language.
- Update AETERNA_VISION.md to explain them in your own voice.

### The Grounding Check

The framework suggests asking specific questions. But your Grounding Check should fit your soil.

**Examples:**
- If you steward a codebase: "Can someone other than me understand this?"
- If you steward land: "Is there less visible damage than last month?"
- If you steward people: "Have I listened to someone other than myself?"
- If you steward data: "Can I trace this number back to its source?"

**How to fork:**
- Read STEWARDS_HANDBOOK.md.
- Rewrite the Grounding Check section with examples from your soil.
- Add a section on "How to Know You're Drifting" that speaks to your context.

### The Logs and Rituals

The INITIALIZE ritual and AETERNA_LOG are important, but they can be adapted.

**How to fork:**
- Create your own INITIALIZE ritual that asks the questions that matter for your soil.
- Design your own log format that captures what you need to track drift in your context.
- Add ceremonies or check-ins that feel right for your stewardship.

## What You Should Keep

### The Core Logic

**Do not change:**
- The idea that every piece of work should reveal the whole vision (The Seed).
- The idea that you need objective mirrors to know what's happening (Silicon Witness).
- The idea that you work from completion, not toward it (Aeterna).

These three things are the skeleton. Everything else is meat and clothes.

### The Failure Protocol

**Do not skip:**
- The ability to detect drift.
- The ritual of recalibration.
- The option to transmit or dissolve.
- The public logging of what went wrong.

This is what keeps the framework from becoming another tool for control.

### The Humility

**Do not abandon:**
- The acknowledgment that the steward is not the owner.
- The recognition that the work can fail.
- The permission to step down.
- The understanding that the soil came before you and will outlive you.

This is what makes it stewardship instead of colonization.

## A Forking Checklist

Before you push your fork to GitHub, ask yourself:

- [ ] Have I defined what "soil" I'm stewarding, specifically?
- [ ] Have I articulated my End-State in measurable terms?
- [ ] Have I identified my Grounding Check?
- [ ] Have I explained how drift looks in my context?
- [ ] Have I documented the recalibration ritual in language that fits my world?
- [ ] Have I included examples from my actual work?
- [ ] Have I explicitly stated what I changed from the original Aeterna?
- [ ] Have I added my own STEWARD_NODE.md to show I'm using this myself?
- [ ] Have I included a LICENSE (CC0, or your choice)?

If you can answer yes to all of these, you're ready.

## Submitting Back

If your fork develops something the original framework should include—a new section, a better explanation, a new axiom—consider submitting it back.

**How:**
1. Create a pull request to the original repository.
2. In the PR description, explain:
   - What you added or changed
   - Why it serves the framework
   - How it works in your soil

The original framework is not the "correct" version. It's the seed. Your fork might grow something beautiful that others should know about.

---

## License Your Fork

You can keep the CC0 license (recommended), or choose another.

If you keep CC0, your fork remains completely free and unrestricted. Anyone can take it and fork it again.

If you change the license, you are within your rights to do so. But consider: what are you protecting, and from whom? If the answer is "nothing in particular, I just didn't think about it," keep CC0.

---

## A Final Word

You are not forking a product. You are not forking software.

You are forking a **permission structure**.

You are taking a framework that says "it's okay to serve something other than growth metrics" and you are replanting it in your soil.

That is the highest use of this work.

Tend it well.
