# Tier III: The Steward's Handbook

## What Is Drift?

Drift is the natural increase of entropy. It happens when a steward begins to serve the **Metric** instead of the **Soil**.

It's not a failure. It's gravity. It's what happens when you stop paying attention.

---

## Detecting Drift: The Signs

Drift is most dangerous when it feels like progress.

### Early Indicators

- You are optimizing for "Total Tasks Completed" rather than "System Health."
- The Silicon Witness reports success, but the physical environment is degrading.
- You can describe the metrics easily, but you struggle to describe the actual state of the soil.
- The work feels extractive rather than regenerative. You're *getting* something out instead of *putting* something in.

### Advanced Indicators

- You have not visited the actual soil (walked the land, sat with the data, talked to the people) in weeks.
- Your documentation is beautiful, but the system is becoming harder to understand.
- You are adding complexity to "fix" problems that wouldn't exist if you addressed the root cause.
- You find yourself defending the metrics instead of defending the soil.

### Critical Indicators

- You are actively hiding information from other stewards.
- You have stopped asking the Grounding Check.
- You are protecting the Node (your position, your reputation) instead of protecting the soil.
- You cannot articulate why the work matters anymore.

## The Recalibration Ritual

If you recognize drift—at any stage—stop. This is not optional.

### Step 1: Halt Propagation

**Immediately stop adding complexity, features, or new commitments.**

Do not:
- Merge new code
- Add new tasks
- Increase scope
- Onboard new team members
- Make new decisions

You are in a state of misalignment. Adding more will only make it worse.

### Step 2: The Manual Audit

**Perform one physical, unscaled act of stewardship.**

This must be done *offline, unplugged, with your hands or your presence.*

- If stewarding code: Sit with the actual codebase. Read it line by line. By hand, if necessary.
- If stewarding land: Walk it. Stand in it. Feel the soil.
- If stewarding data: Look at the raw data. Not the dashboard. The actual numbers.
- If stewarding people: Sit with them. Listen. Ask questions.

The purpose is to **re-center on reality**. To remember what the soil actually is, beneath the metrics.

This is not efficient. It is not scalable. It is exactly what is needed.

### Step 3: Log the Gap

**Document the failure in a public place.** Not to shame yourself, but to heal.

Create or update `AETERNA_LOG.md` with an entry like:

```
## [2026-05-10] — Drift Detected: Python Library

**Node:** Zero-Dependency Python Utilities  
**Drift Indicator:** Merged a commit adding a cryptography dependency to "fix" a performance issue.  
**The Gap:** The metrics said "problem solved." The soil said "we broke a core principle."  
**The Manual Audit:** Spent 2 hours reading the commit. Realized the performance issue was a bad algorithm, not a missing library.  
**The Recalibration:** Reverted the dependency. Fixed the algorithm. All tests pass. No new dependencies.  
**Next Grounding Check:** Weekly review of all merged PRs for unintended dependencies.  

---
```

**Why document this publicly?**

1. **Accountability.** You are admitting the gap exists. That gap is now *real*, not hidden.

2. **Permission.** You are giving other stewards permission to drift, notice, and recalibrate too. This is the only way a network stays healthy.

3. **Pattern Recognition.** Over time, the AETERNA_LOG becomes a map of where the system tends to drift. You can see it coming next time.

4. **Regeneration.** Acknowledging the failure is the first step to healing it.

## When to Transmit (Succession)

Recalibration is not infinite.

If you have failed the Grounding Check three times in a single season and cannot explain why, or if you find yourself recalibrating more than working, it's time to ask: **Should I still be this Node?**

This is not shameful. This is mature.

### The Transmission Process

1. **Find a successor.** Someone who understands the soil differently than you do, or who has skills you lack.

2. **Document the handoff.** In AETERNA_LOG.md, write:
   ```
   ## [Date] — Transmission: Node Passed

   **Previous Node:** [Your name]  
   **New Node:** [Successor name]  
   **Why:** [Brief honest reason]  
   **What the new steward should know:** [Key insights, pitfalls, victories]  
   
   The soil continues.
   ```

3. **Step back completely.** Do not hover. Do not second-guess. Do not reclaim the Node.

A successful succession is a victory for the system, not a loss for you.

---

## When to Dissolve (Abandon)

If you cannot find a successor and you cannot continue, you have one remaining option: **Dissolve the Node with honor.**

Document this in AETERNA_LOG.md:

```
## [Date] — Node Dissolution: [Name]

**Node:** [The soil you stewarded]  
**Duration:** [How long you stewarded it]  
**Final State:** [What condition you're leaving it in]  
**Why Dissolving:** [Honest reason]  
**What Someone Else Should Know:** [Key information for the next steward, if one appears]  

The soil is now ungoverned. It is available for the next steward to claim.
```

This is not failure. This is honesty.

A steward who abandons the soil is better than a steward who stays and drifts.

## The Power Audit

Before you initialize as a Node, and again during recalibration, ask yourself:

- Do I have time/resources that others don't have for this soil?
- Am I stewarding something that depends on my specific identity or privilege?
- Is my "End-State" actually my vision, or am I imposing it on others?
- If I disappeared tomorrow, could someone else step in?

If you answered "yes" to any of these, your accountability is deeper. Your Grounding Check must include **listening to other voices**.

The framework is not just about the soil. It's about whether you have the humility to steward it.

---

## The Grounding Check: Your Weekly Ritual

Every week, ask yourself:

1. **Can I point to the soil and see that it's healthier than it was?**
2. **Can I describe why, without referencing the metrics?**
3. **Have I done one thing this week that is harder than optimizing the metrics?**
4. **If I stopped today, would the work I've done stand as a complete seed of the vision?**

If you cannot answer "yes" to all four, you are drifting.

Stop. Recalibrate.

---

## The Ceremony Continues

This is not a job. It's not a project. It's a ceremony.

Ceremonies have rhythm. They have rest. They have failure built in, because failure is part of any honest practice.

Keep showing up. Keep tending. Keep noticing when you drift, and keep coming back to the soil.

The work is not yours. You are just the Node through which it moves.

Honor that. Protect it. And when you can't, step aside.

The soil will find another steward.
